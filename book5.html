Prologue: The Tide

The lighthouse had been dark for thirty years. That was the official story. The story written in the town archives, printed in the tourist brochures, and muttered by the fishermen who drank too much at The Rusty Anchor.

But Clara knew different. Because Clara was watching it burn.

Not with fire. Fire would have been clean. Fire would have been loud. This was a cold burn, a silent, white phosphorescence that seemed to eat the stone from the inside out. It pulsed like a heartbeat, slow and rhythmic, matching the pounding in her own chest.

She tightened her grip on the railing of the cliff walk. The wind here didn't just blow; it bit. It carried the taste of salt and... something else. Ash? No, darker. Old earth and things that had been buried too long.

About the Book

New York Times Bestseller

Clara Reed has spent her entire life running from the quiet. In the bustling streets of London, she found comfort in the chaos, a symphony of distraction that kept her own thoughts at bay.

But when a mysterious inheritance brings her back to the coastal village of Blackwood Bay, the silence she feared becomes her only companion. There, amidst the salt air and the crashing waves, she discovers a journal left behind by a man she never knew—her father.

The Echo of Silence is a lyrical exploration of forgiveness, heritage, and the courage it takes to listen to your own heart when the world stops making noise.

About the Author

Elias Vance is an award-winning novelist.

Born in a small fishing village in Maine, Elias developed a fascination with the ocean and its secrets at a young age. His debut novel, The Light Keeper, won the Pen/Hemingway Award.

He currently lives in Brooklyn with his wife and two dogs, spending his days writing and his nights searching for the perfect cup of coffee.
